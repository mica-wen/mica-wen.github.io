Northeastern Electric Racing (NER): Electric Motor Cooling System

Powertrain thermal management · Northeastern Electric Racing (2025–Present)

Overview

Designing and validating liquid cooling subsystems for Northeastern Electric Racing's electric vehicle powertrain, including coolant circuit layout, component selection, and thermal performance testing.

What I Did

  • Validated liquid cooling circuits for BLDC motors, characterizing pump flow rates, radiator capacity, and coolant line pressure drops
  • Executed thermal load testing across multiple duty cycles, logging temperature and flow data to benchmark system performance
  • Quantified radiator heat rejection rates and correlated results with analytical heat transfer models
  • Applied first-principles thermodynamics to size components and inform design trade-offs for the cooling loop
  • Modeled a swirl pot reservoir in Fusion 360, optimizing geometry for air separation and coolant de-aeration; prepared for SLA 3D printing
  • Integrated cooling system packaging within vehicle chassis constraints in coordination with the powertrain sub-team
Fusion 360 SolidWorks SLA 3D Printing Thermal Analysis Heat Transfer

Computer Vision Drone Localization

Undergraduate research · Prof. Rifat Sipahi, Northeastern University (2025–Present)

Overview

Building a vision-based localization pipeline for autonomous drone navigation using stereo cameras, 3D terrain reconstruction, and ground-truth validation with motion capture.

What I Did

  • Deployed a stereo-vision localization pipeline on NVIDIA Jetson Nano, capturing and processing imagery over a scaled sandbox terrain to simulate aerial flight
  • Evaluated 3D scene reconstruction accuracy using COLMAP structure-from-motion, comparing point clouds against known terrain geometry
  • Performed intrinsic and extrinsic camera calibration and validated localization accuracy against OptiTrack motion capture ground truth
